Methodology, signals & disclaimer

Reference (fair) odds. Each team's championship probability is derived from the consensus sportsbook outright market (DraftKings board via ESPN, Apr 2026), converted from American odds to implied probability and de-vigged (normalised to sum to 100%, removing the ~18% bookmaker overround). Team strength ratings were then calibrated by Monte Carlo so the simulated tournament reproduces those championship probabilities (within ~0.4pp). This is your “market-implied baseline.”

Simulation. Group matches use a Davidson win/draw/loss model; knockout matches a Bradley–Terry win model, over the exact official 2026 bracket including the 8-best-third-place routing (all 495 FIFA Annex C combinations embedded). Locking outcomes conditions the remaining simulation, so championship and round-by-round odds update live.

Edge & signals. Edge (pp) = Reference% − Polymarket%. EV% = Reference / Polymarket − 1 (expected value of buying a YES share at the Polymarket price). BUY when EV ≥ threshold (Polymarket too cheap vs fair), SELL when EV ≤ −threshold. Switch “Edge vs” to Live model to price your specific bracket scenario instead of the static baseline.

Disclaimer. This is an analytical tool, not financial advice. “Fair” odds are only as good as the inputs; prediction-market prices can be right and the book consensus wrong. Markets move — refresh the Polymarket cells with live values before acting, and manage risk yourself.
